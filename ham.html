Down at Sunset Pier, the gulls held their weekly council beneath the boardwalk. Alana, the self-appointed mayor of the flock, cleared her throat and squawked, "Attention, everyone! Operation Lunch begins in five minutes. Positions!" The plan was simple: swoop, snatch, scatter. Today, however, a rookie named Frankie had other ideas. While the veterans targeted a family's sandwich platter, Frankie fixed his beady eyes on something far more ambitious—a toddler's melting ice cream cone. "Frankie, no!" Alana screeched. "That's not protocol! We do sandwiches on Tuesdays!" But Frankie was already airborne, wings pumping with reckless determination. He dive-bombed the unsuspecting toddler, snatched the ice cream clean off its cone, and executed what witnesses later described as a "victory barrel roll" over the crowd. Chaos erupted. Seventeen other gulls, sensing an opportunity, abandoned their sandwich mission entirely and swarmed every cone within a hundred yards. Children wailed. Parents shouted. One man lost his hot dog to a gull who wasn't even hungry—just caught up in the excitement. Alana watched from a lamppost, shaking her feathery head as Frankie landed beside her, ice cream smeared across his beak like a badge of honor. "Rookie mistake," Alana muttered. "You've started a revolution." Commander Barnaby, a scarred seagull, briefed his elite squad on the boardwalk roof. "Listen up, flock. No garbage for lunch today. We are targeting the ultimate prize: vanilla ice cream."Private Pip saluted with a wing. Their target was a toddler holding a towering waffle cone. The kid was a walking hazard—clumsy, sticky, and completely unguarded."Execute Operation Brain Freeze!" Barnaby squawked.Pip dove first, acting as the decoy. He soared low, screaming like a broken bagpipe. The terrified toddler ducked, dropping his guard. Barnaby seized the moment. He swooped in from the sun, his beak open wide. With the precision of a fighter jet, he scooped the top scoop right off the cone.Success! But victory was short-lived. Barnaby swallowed the frozen dairy treat in one massive, greedy gulp. Suddenly, his eyes went wide. His wings froze mid-flap. A brutal brain freeze struck his tiny avian skull.He crashed straight into a nearby sandcastle, groaning in agony. Pip landed beside him, asset secured but commander down."Was it worth it, sir?" Pip whispered.Barnaby blinked through the pain, a white mustache of cream on his beak. "Absolutely. Tomorrow, we go for the fries."
